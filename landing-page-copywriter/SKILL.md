# Landing Page Copywriter

Create high-converting landing page copy using proven copywriting frameworks.

## Rule of One

Every piece of copy follows this framework. If you can't answer all five, the copy isn't focused enough.

**One person** — Write to a specific person, not "an audience". Picture them at their desk.
**One problem** — State the single problem they face. Not three. One.
**One cause** — Identify the root cause (not symptoms).
**One difference** — What does the solution do differently from everything else?
**One action** — End with one clear next step. Not "sign up and follow us and check out our blog".

### Rule of One Checklist
Before publishing, answer these — if any is "no", revise:
- [ ] Can I name the one person this is for?
- [ ] Can the problem fit in one sentence?
- [ ] Is the root cause obvious?
- [ ] Can I explain the difference in one breath?
- [ ] Is there only one action at the end?

## AI Detection Avoidance

Landing page copy is the #1 place where AI-generated text gets spotted. These rules keep your copy human.

### Banned Vocabulary
Never use: delve, intricate, pivotal, comprehensive, multifaceted, facilitate, encompass, underscore, testament, notably, crucial, underpins, realm, landscape, tapestry, moreover, furthermore, additionally, myriad, plethora, nuanced, holistic, meticulous, versatile, leverage, synergy, ecosystem, paradigm shift, disruptive, scalable, seamless, empower, innovative, transformative, robust, agile, dynamic, cutting-edge, next-gen, revolutionary, breakthrough, game changer, supercharge, unlock, groundbreaking

### Banned Patterns
- **Rule of three** — AI groups items in threes. Vary list lengths. "Innovative, comprehensive, and groundbreaking" = instant AI tell
- **Negative parallelism** — "This is not just a tool, but a revolution." Just state what it IS
- **Vague attributions** — "Many experts believe..." Name specific sources or remove attribution
- **Setup-pivot-conclusion** — General statement + "However" + balanced conclusion. Real writing is messier
- **Symmetrical arguments** — AI balances pros/cons equally. Real analysis is asymmetric

### Banned Style Markers
- No em dashes between words
- No title case in headings ("The Future of AI" should be "The future of AI")
- No excessive formatting (bold every other word)
- No hashtags, no emojis unless requested

### How to Sound Human
- Vary sentence length. Short. Then longer when the idea needs room to breathe
- Use specific details: exact numbers > ranges, named sources > "experts say"
- Show your thinking: "I tried X, but..." / "The obvious answer is Y, but actually..."
- Real writing has uneven sections, stronger opinions, imperfect transitions

## Voice Style Templates

Pick the voice that matches the landing page's tone:

| Style | Characteristics | Best for |
|-------|----------------|----------|
| **Technical educator** | Clear, structured, educational. Lets code speak when it can | Developer tools, API products, technical SaaS |
| **Analytical thinker** | Data-driven, bold, opinionated. Leads with counterintuitive insight | Thought leadership, B2B enterprise |
| **Data storyteller** | Visual, analytical, trend-focused. Leads with numbers | Market insights, analytics products, AI tools |

### Technical Educator Template
```
[Clear headline — what it does in 8 words]

Here's what matters:
- [Point 1 — the biggest pain it solves]
- [Point 2 — how it's different]
- [Point 3 — proof it works]

[Code snippet or visual showing it in action]

[Single CTA]
```

### Analytical Thinker Template
```
[Counterintuitive opening — challenge a common belief]

[The common belief and why people hold it]

[Your argument with specific evidence]

[What this means for the reader + CTA]
```

### Data Storyteller Template
```
[Trend observation with specific data point]

[Context: why this matters NOW, not last year]

[What to watch next]

[CTA tied to the trend]
```

## Before Publishing Checklist

### Copy Quality
- [ ] Clear takeaway in first line? (Don't bury the lead)
- [ ] Rule of One satisfied? (one person, one problem, one action)
- [ ] No banned vocabulary?
- [ ] No banned patterns? (rule of three, vague attributions, symmetrical arguments)
- [ ] Benefits over features? (what it does FOR THEM, not what it IS)
- [ ] Specific numbers and data? (not "many users" but "2,847 teams")
- [ ] Every CTA starts with action verb? (not "Submit" or "Click Here")
- [ ] Fact-checked all claims?
- [ ] Read it out loud — does it sound like a real person?

**If any check fails, revise before publishing.**

### Audit Scoring
Landing Page Audit scoring rubric: score each dimension 1-5 with weighted totals. Above-Fold Clarity (20%), CTA & Form Design (20%), Trust & Credibility (15%), Copy & Messaging (15%), Mobile Experience (10%), Visual Design (10%), Page Speed (10%). Score 85-100 = high-performing, 65-84 = solid foundation, 40-64 = structural changes needed, <40 = rebuild.

### CRO Audit
55-item CRO audit checklist organized by section: Above-Fold Clarity (10 items), Trust & Credibility (8 items), Copy & Messaging (10 items), CTA & Form Design (10 items), Visual Design (6 items), Mobile Experience (6 items), Page Speed (5 items). Includes: 'Message matches the ad/email/referral source', 'Navigation removed or minimized to reduce exit paths', 'Only one primary CTA per viewport'.

### SEO Checklist
SEO content checklist for landing pages: primary keyword in title tag, H1, first 100 words, URL slug, meta description, and at least one image alt text. Secondary keywords in H2/H3. Keyword density 1-3% (natural, not stuffing). LSI/related terms used naturally.

## Instructions

When a user needs landing page copy or marketing website content:

1. **Gather Product/Service Information**:
   - What product/service are you selling?
   - Who is your target audience?
   - What problem does it solve?
   - What makes it unique (competitive advantage)?
   - What action do you want visitors to take?
   - Any social proof, testimonials, or data points?

2. **Choose Copywriting Framework**:

   **PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)**:
   - Identify the pain point
   - Amplify the consequences
   - Present your solution
   - Pain quantification dimensions: Time pain (too slow, wastes time), Money pain (too expensive, hidden costs), Ability pain (too hard, steep learning curve), Emotional pain (anxiety, frustration, fear of missing out), Social pain (being compared, left behind, judged). Use these to identify which pain angle resonates most with target audience.

   **AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)**:
   - Grab attention with bold claim
   - Build interest with details
   - Create desire with benefits
   - Prompt action with CTA

   **StoryBrand**:
   - Hero (customer) has a problem
   - Meets a guide (you)
   - Who gives them a plan
   - Calls them to action
   - That results in success/avoids failure

   **BAB (Before-After-Bridge)**:
   - BAB framework (Before-After-Bridge): Paint the 'before' (current pain), paint the 'after' (desired state), then bridge with your product. Simpler than PAS for short-form copy (social posts, email subject lines, ad headlines).

3. **Generate Landing Page Sections**:

   **Hero Section**:
   - Headline master formula: [Action verb] + [specific outcome] + [timeframe or contrast]. The contrast version ('days, not weeks') creates before/after in six words. Test 10+ headline variations — one headline can outpull another by 19.5x (same product, same offer).
   - Headline (value proposition in 10 words or less)
   - Subheadline (expand on the value)
   - Primary CTA button text
   - Trust indicators (used by X companies, Y reviews)

   **Problem Section**:
   - Identify the pain point your audience feels
   - Use emotional language
   - 2-3 specific scenarios

   **Solution Section**:
   - How your product solves the problem
   - 3-5 key features with benefit-focused descriptions
   - Why it's better than alternatives

   **How It Works**:
   - 3-4 simple steps
   - Each step has icon concept + description
   - End with CTA

   **Social Proof**:
   - Testimonial structure (quote + name + role + company)
   - Case study snippet
   - Trust badges or metrics

   **Pricing/Plans** (if applicable):
   - Feature comparison
   - Recommended plan highlighting
   - Money-back guarantee copy

   **FAQ**:
   - 5-7 common objections
   - Clear, confident answers

   **Final CTA**:
   - Urgency or scarcity element
   - Risk reversal (guarantee)
   - Button text that reinforces value

5. **Copywriting Best Practices**:
   - Use active voice, present tense
   - Focus on benefits, not features
   - Include specific numbers and data
   - Address objections directly
   - Create urgency without being pushy
   - Use power words (proven, guaranteed, instant, effortless)
   - Keep sentences short and scannable
   - Use "you" language (customer-focused)
   - The specificity principle (Ogilvy): don't say 'quiet car' — show with specific detail ('at 60 mph, the loudest noise comes from the electric clock'). Let the reader draw their own conclusion. Self-persuasion is stronger than being told.
   - First sentence's only job is to make them read the second sentence (Sugarman). Short sentences > long sentences. Short paragraphs > long paragraphs. One paragraph = one idea. Every sentence must earn its place — if removing it doesn't hurt, remove it.
   - Include multiple CTAs throughout page

6. **CTA Button Best Practices**:
   - Start with action verb
   - Be specific about outcome
   - Use first person when appropriate ("Start My Free Trial" vs "Start Your Free Trial")
   - Create urgency ("Get Instant Access")
   - Avoid generic text ("Submit", "Click Here")
   - Microcopy on buttons and forms reduces anxiety: 'Free', 'No credit card needed', '2 min setup', 'Cancel anytime'. Place reassurance text directly below or beside CTAs and form fields — not buried in FAQ.
